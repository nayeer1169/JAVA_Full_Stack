02 — Common Attacks

Know Your
Enemy

Threat actors exploit predictable human behavior and unpatched systems. These are the weapons they wield against you every single day.

01

Malware

Malicious software — viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware — designed to infiltrate, damage, or hold your system hostage for ransom.

Risk
Critical

02

Ransomware

Encrypts your files and demands payment for the decryption key. Average ransom demand exceeded $1.5 million in 2024.

Risk
Severe

03

Man-in-the-Middle

An attacker secretly intercepts and relays communications between two parties, eavesdropping or altering data in transit.

Risk
High

04

DDoS Attack

Floods your server with traffic from thousands of compromised machines, rendering your service completely unavailable.

Risk
High

05

SQL Injection

Attackers insert malicious code into database queries, bypassing authentication and extracting sensitive records from databases.

Risk
Moderate

06

Social Engineering

Psychological manipulation that tricks individuals into revealing confidential information or performing security-compromising actions.

Risk
Severe

03 — Password Security

Your First
Line of Defence

81% of data breaches involve stolen or weak passwords. A strong password strategy is the single most impactful step you can take right now.

Enter a password above

  • ✕ At least 12 characters long
  • ✕ Contains uppercase letters
  • ✕ Contains lowercase letters
  • ✕ Contains numbers
  • ✕ Contains special characters (!@#$…)

Password Best Practices

01

Use a Password Manager

Tools like Bitwarden or 1Password generate and store unique passwords for every site. Never reuse passwords.

02

Enable Multi-Factor Auth

MFA blocks 99.9% of automated attacks. Use an authenticator app, not SMS, for maximum security.

03

Use Passphrases

String 4–5 random words together: "purple-volcano-7-lantern" is more secure and memorable than "P@ssw0rd".

04

Audit Breached Passwords

Check haveibeenpwned.com regularly. If your email appears in a breach, change those passwords immediately.

04 — Phishing Awareness

Don't Take
the Bait

3.4 billion phishing emails are sent every day. Learn to identify the hallmarks before you click.

From: security-alert@paypa1.com ⚠ SUSPICIOUS
Subject: ⚡ URGENT: Your account will be suspended in 24 hours!

Dear Valued Customer,

We have detected suspicious activity on your account. Your account will be permanently suspended if you do not verify immediately.

→ Click here to verify your account now

Sincerely,
The Security Team

Red Flags Identified

  • 🚩

    Artificial Urgency

    Phrases like "24 hours" and "act now" are designed to stop you from thinking critically.

  • 🚩

    Spoofed Sender Domain

    "paypa1.com" vs "paypal.com" — a single character swap, easily missed.

  • 🚩

    Generic Greeting

    Legitimate companies address you by name. "Valued Customer" signals a mass phishing campaign.

  • 🚩

    Suspicious Links

    Always hover to preview a link's true destination before clicking. Never click without checking.

  • 🚩

    Threats & Fear

    Threatening account suspension, legal action, or financial loss is a classic social engineering tactic.

05 — Security Tips

Your Digital
Armour

Implement these practices to drastically reduce your attack surface. Small habits compound into fortress-level security.

Update Regularly

Enable automatic updates for OS, browsers, and apps. Patches close known vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit.

Critical

Use a VPN

Encrypt your internet connection on public Wi-Fi. A VPN hides your traffic from eavesdroppers and masks your IP address.

High

Backup Your Data

Follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 different media, 1 offsite. If ransomware hits, backups are your ultimate recovery plan.

Critical

Enable Firewall

Your OS firewall blocks unauthorized network connections. Keep it enabled and configure it to deny all inbound by default.

High

Multi-Factor Auth

Add a second verification layer to every important account. Even if your password is stolen, attackers can't get in without the second factor.

Critical

Secure Browsing

Use HTTPS-only sites, keep an ad blocker active, disable unnecessary browser extensions, and clear cookies regularly.

Moderate
